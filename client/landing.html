Our Philosophy

Understanding Sign Language

This is not a technical spec — it is the philosophical foundation of our entire project. Before writing a single line of code, our team studied the linguistics of sign language. What we learned fundamentally shaped our architecture.

Most people assume sign language is universal, artificial, and incomplete.

All three assumptions are wrong.

Myth Busted

Not Artificial

Sign languages were not invented by anyone. They emerged naturally within Deaf communities over centuries, just like spoken languages. They have their own grammar, syntax, and idiomatic expressions.

The Korean Sign Language expression for “catching a smell” has a meaning no hearing Korean would intuit without learning KSL.

Myth Busted

Not Universal

There are over 300+ distinct sign languages worldwide. ASL (American Sign Language) is completely different from BSL (British Sign Language), even though both countries speak English.

ASL BSL KSL TSL + 296 more
Myth Busted

Not Incomplete

Sign languages are equal to spoken languages in structural complexity and expressive power. In some domains — spatial relationships, simultaneous information — they are actually more expressive.

A raised eyebrow in ASL can change a statement into a question — facial expressions carry grammatical meaning.

How This Shapes Our Design

Misconception

“Sign language is universal”

Our Response

Modular architecture — each sign language (ASL, KSL, TSL, BSL...) gets its own recognition model, swappable without rebuilding the system.

Misconception

“Sign language is a helper tool”

Our Response

Equal bridge, not assistive tool — sign and speech are two equally valid input/output modalities. Neither is primary.

Misconception

“Sign language is less expressive”

Our Response

Bidirectional emotion preservation — we preserve emotional information in both directions (tone/pitch in speech; facial expressions in sign).

Built with Responsibility

Accessibility and inclusion are not add-ons to our project — they ARE the project.

Our system is built on the understanding that sign language is a complete, natural language. We treat sign and speech as two equally valid modalities, not as “primary” and “assistive.”

Privacy

Video streams processed in real-time, no permanent storage by default. User-controlled data retention. Encrypted in transit and at rest. Azure Key Vault for all secrets.

Transparency

AI confidence scores displayed to users (e.g., “This sign was recognized with 85% confidence”). Clear indication when AI is uncertain.

Fairness

300+ sign languages acknowledged as independent natural languages. Modular design prevents cultural/linguistic bias. Tested across diverse skin tones, signing styles, and lighting.

Content Safety

Azure Content Safety filters harmful content in real-time transcription. Automatic detection and removal of inappropriate content across all output channels.

Inclusive Design

Following Microsoft Inclusive Design principles. UI designed for accessibility from the ground up, not retrofitted. Bidirectional emotion preservation for ALL participants.

Linguistic Respect

Sign language treated as natural language with full grammar and expressiveness, not as gesture-based shorthand. Modular architecture respects each sign language's unique identity.
